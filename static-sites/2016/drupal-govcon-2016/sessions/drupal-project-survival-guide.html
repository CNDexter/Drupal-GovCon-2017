DRUPAL PROJECT SURVIVAL GUIDE

Session Track: 

Business and Strategy

The purpose of this track is two-fold - one angle helps you to understand the advantages of Drupal from an organizational leadership angle and the other is to highlight the great Drupal sites and projects you're working on and to show what's possible. These sessions are business focused, and will be non-technical.

Speaker(s): 
sayasone@gmail.com

Workshop—Drupal Project Survival Guide

Dr. Saya Sone will offer a workshop entitled, “DRUPAL PROJECT SURVIVAL GUIDE” to help Drupal teams build their own survival guide. We all know the basic survival needs from Maslow’s human needs hierarchy as follows.

  • Physiological needs
  • Safety needs
  • Social contact
  • Self-esteem
  • Self-actualization

Steve McConnell said project has a similar hierarchy as human’s needs. Project needs for the high self-esteem of individual team members in order to satisfy the high-level needs. Based on Steve’s suggestion, the project survival guide is as follows:

  • Project not cancelled, team not fired and adequate physical working conditions
  • Meeting personal promises for schedule and functionality
  • Healthy team dynamics
  • Feeling productive, belief in project’s importance
  • Ongoing professional development

This workshop will use state-of-the-art Brain Science (back-of-the-room) techniques to help people identify from human needs and transform them into project needs. Our common project goal is to deliver project to meet customer needs. We also expect customer to pay attention to project team’s needs. The majority of the time will be used to build the Drupal project survival guide based on the hierarchy needs.  Audience may use the following questions to gather ideas.

  • Requirement: Do we have clear requirement
  • Plan: Does the project have any software development plan?
  • Control: Does the project have a feedback channel to understand issues?
  • Player: Does the project team have all he technical expertise need to complete the project?
  • Risk: Does the project have a risk list?
  • Results. The evidence shows if team has built a survival guide can better serve their customers,  work with their team, and increase productivity for the organizations. This was often called the wisdom of teams and crowds, power of two, team synergy, and other monikers describing how ordinary teams of people can collectively achieve the benefits of higher-performing individuals acting alone.

Workshop Outline

  • Introduction [10 min]. Briefly describe Maslow’s human needs model and what we would like to build our software team survival needs.
  • Interaction [10 min]. Audience can practice to build their own Maslow model and share with the group.
  • Physiological needs
  • Safety needs
  • Social contact
  • Self-esteem
  • Self-actualization
  • Group Exercise [20 min]. Divide the participants into one or more teams have them appoint a coach, hand out a project scenario of a team struggling with poor performance, and allow the participants to whiteboard a survival solution using the Steve McConnell’s model.
    • Project not cancelled, team not fired and adequate physical working conditions
    • Meeting personal promises for schedule and functionality
    • Healthy team dynamics
    • Feeling productive, belief in project's importance
    • Ongoing professional development
  • Discussion [15 min]. Allow the teams to stand up and describe their survival guide. Hand out a scorecard in advance, and have participants rate the usefulness of the workshop and instructor delivery.

Workshop Facilitator’s Biography

Dr. Saya Sone is an adjunct faculty at VIU and an Enterprise Agile Coach, specializing in the Scaled Agile Academy's Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe), Scrum, Kanban, Lean Startups, Lean Canvas, and other major Lean and Agile Methods, Frameworks, and Practices. She's an experienced organizational change leader and she's successfully led multiple Scrum roll-outs for more than a decade.

She's been a technical leader and manager of large and complex public sector information systems projects across the entire life cycle in the public and private sector for over 15 years. She's served in a variety of roles such as Senior Project Manager, Agile Program Manager, Agile Coach, Agile Trainer, Scrum Master, etc. Some of her major clients have included the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS), U.S. Postal Service (USPS), U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC), and many others. She's worked for high-profile organizations, such as Freddie Mac, Agilex, Airlines Reporting Corporation (ARC), Computer Sciences Corporation (CSC), America On Line (AOL), British Telecom (BT), and Blackstone Technology Group.

She has won multiple awards for leadership, project management, and Agile methods across a variety of different organizations. She's a frequent speaker at professional societies and conferences, and has also taught many Lean and Agile training courses and workshops. She co-authored the first textbook on return on investment of Agile Methods, entitled, "Business Value of Agile Methods: Maximizing ROI with Just-in-Time Processes and Documentation." She specializes in agile project performance metrics, measures, and models, and is highly-skilled in Earned Value Management (EVM).

She is also a certified executive life coach, a SAFe Program Consultant (SPC), a Project Management Institute (PMI) Certified Project Management Professional, Certified Scrum Master (CSM), Professional Scrum Master (PSM), Certified Scrum Professional (CSP), and Certified Six Sigma Lean Black Belt Professional. She holds a Master’s Degree in Communications, Master’s Degree in Project Management, and Doctor of Business Administration (DBA). She did one of the first doctoral dissertations on Agile Project Management (APM), before most organizations were even aware of this paradigm. She also teaches graduate courses in Washington, DC.
